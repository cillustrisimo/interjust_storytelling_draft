The History and Use of Universal Jurisdiction

Understanding the laws InterJust uses to fight atrocity crimes, and their context.

A joint project: Berkeley Responsibility Lab x Project Meridian

While XX U.N. Member States have laws that allow them to investigate and prosecute crimes committed beyond their own borders (universal jurisdiction), few have actually done so. Project Meridian is dedicated to mobilizing States to use their laws to hold perpetrators accountable for the most serious international crimes, so that all survivors can realize access to justice. But, what is universal jurisdiction?

Universal jurisdiction is a legal principle which allows States to prosecute the most serious international crimes – war crimes, crimes against humanity, genocide, and the crime of aggression – regardless of where those crimes were committed, the nationality of the perpetrator, and the nationality of the victim. Lawyers and advocates have been working to develop universal jurisdiction as a mechanism to pursue justice for survivors of mass atrocities since the Second World War.
